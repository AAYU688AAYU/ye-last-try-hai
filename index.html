Understanding Color Blindness

Types of Color Blindness:

Protanopia (Red-blind)
Difficulty distinguishing between red and green colors due to missing L-cones (long wavelength).
Deuteranopia (Green-blind)
Most common form, affects ability to see green light due to missing M-cones (medium wavelength).
Tritanopia (Blue-blind)
Rare condition affecting blue-yellow discrimination due to missing S-cones (short wavelength).
Monochromacy
Complete color blindness where only shades of gray are perceived.

Note: This AI diagnostic tool analyzes facial features and eye characteristics to detect potential color vision deficiencies. For official diagnosis, consult an eye care professional.
